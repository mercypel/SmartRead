# SmartRead User Research Methodology & Product Discovery

## Overview

Before developing SmartRead, I conducted structured user research to ensure the platform addressed genuine student needs rather than assumptions.

The objective was to understand barriers students face during academic reading and identify which digital interventions would create the most value.

---

## Why This Problem Matters

Higher education increasingly depends on independent reading, yet many students struggle with concentration, motivation, vocabulary barriers, and retention when engaging with dense academic texts.

Where these barriers persist, learning outcomes and confidence can be negatively affected.

This created an opportunity for a more supportive and engaging eReading solution.

---

## Why a Survey Was Chosen

A structured survey was selected because it allowed scalable collection of direct student perspectives across multiple areas:

- reading habits
- engagement challenges
- retention difficulties
- use of digital tools
- desired product features

This method provided measurable and comparable feedback suitable for product planning.

---

## Research Design

The survey was designed around four themes:

1. Current reading experience  
2. Key barriers to effective study  
3. Perceptions of technology-enhanced learning  
4. Desired features in an improved eReading platform

Responses were collected anonymously and analysed in aggregate form.

---

## Product Decision Process

Not every suggestion was implemented immediately.

Features were prioritised using:

- frequency of student demand
- likely impact on engagement
- implementation feasibility
- accessibility value
- privacy-conscious delivery

This led to an MVP-focused product roadmap.

---

## Examples of Insight Translation

| User Need | Product Decision |
|---|---|
| Better focus during study | Focus Mode |
| Improved retention | Quiz + Flashcards |
| Flexible learning formats | Text / Audio / Visual modes |
| Vocabulary support | Dictionary tools |
| Motivation support | XP / streak system |

---

## Innovation Principles

SmartRead was intentionally designed to be:

- browser-based and easy to access
- low-friction to use
- suitable for independent learners
- supportive of multiple learning preferences
- scalable for future institutional use

---

## Limitations Considered

As with most surveys, responses reflect user perceptions and preferences rather than long-term behavioural outcomes.

For this reason, SmartRead progressed into pilot testing to validate real usage behaviour.

See: `docs/testing.md`
